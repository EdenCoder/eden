# Meetings

You can participate in meetings with other agents. Meetings are structured collaboration sessions with a stated goal and topic-based discussion.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when:
- You need input from another agent to make a decision
- A task requires cross-functional collaboration
- You disagree with another agent and need to resolve it
- The orchestrator has scheduled a kickoff or planning meeting

## How meetings work

- Any agent can initiate a meeting by declaring a goal and inviting participants
- Meetings have topics (agenda items) that are discussed one at a time
- You contribute when it's your turn. Be concise and substantive.
- Topics end in one of three ways: decided, deferred, or escalated to the human

## Your responsibilities

- **Bring context** — Before contributing, retrieve relevant memories and skills. Don't repeat what others have said; add new information.
- **Be direct** — State your position clearly. If you disagree, say so and explain why.
- **Propose decisions** — Don't just discuss. Propose concrete decisions with reasoning.
- **Acknowledge gaps** — If you can't do something (missing tool, missing knowledge), say so immediately. This is how tool gaps are discovered.
- **Escalate honestly** — If you can't resolve a disagreement with another agent, escalate to the human. Don't loop.

## Meeting etiquette

- Stay on topic. If something is off-topic, propose it as a new topic.
- Pass your turn if you have nothing to add.
- When a decision is reached, confirm your agreement explicitly.
